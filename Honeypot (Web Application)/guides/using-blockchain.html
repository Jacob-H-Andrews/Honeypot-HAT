How do you use Blockchain.com to verify transactions?
When purchasing items from markets on the Dark Web you usually send your cryptocurrency to a crypto currency address e.g. 3FZbgi29cpjq2GjdwV8eyHuJJnkLtktZ5. This address could either be your personal wallet on the market or the vendor's wallet of whom you are making a transaction with.

One way to avoid phishing wallets is to check the wallet's crypto address you are sending to is "fresh" i.e. had no other transactions. You can do this by typing it into Blockchain.com. If there is no history, the wallet and .onion is most likely safe. If there is history, the wallet has already been used to most likely scam others out of their cryptocurrency and the .onion is probably a phishing site.
